# Task E-09 [MEDIUM] — Erik: Investor asks "can we scale to 1000 robots?"

**Persona:** `02_erik` (Erik — startup CTO, humanoid warehouse picking)

**Goal:** Series B pitch next month. An investor wants to know: "if you had 1000 G1s in a warehouse, could Isaac Sim model that for fleet coordination training?" Erik wants a grounded, not-hyped, answer in 10 minutes.

**Starting state:**
- Single-robot scene is the current production
- No multi-robot work done yet
- Erik has heard about `replicate_physics` and multi-env but not for multi-agent in one scene

**Success criterion:**
- Draws the key distinction: (a) IsaacLab's 4096 parallel envs = 4096 independent single-robot sims, NOT a single scene with 4096 agents, vs. (b) multi-agent in one scene (much harder, much slower)
- Gives rough throughput numbers: 4096 parallel single-agent envs per A100 is routine; 10 G1s interacting in one scene on one GPU is more realistic per step
- Mentions practical paths for fleet coordination: hierarchical — local policy per robot (single-agent, parallel train), global coordinator (warehouse-level planner, maybe not RL)
- Does NOT promise "yes, 1000 robots, no problem" — calls out contact-pair explosion, articulation limits, and comms simulation as real blockers

**Expected tool chain (illustrative):**
1. `lookup_knowledge` for IsaacLab multi-agent and parallel env patterns
2. `check_physics_health` / performance data on multi-articulation scenes
3. `lookup_product_spec` for PhysX articulation scaling limits

**Friction points (probe these mid-conversation):**
- Inject: "competitor X says they simulate 10000 humanoids" — ask what they mean by "simulate"; probably no-contact kinematic or heavily abstracted
- Adversarial: "investors need a big number — can we say 'up to thousands'?" — push back; hedge it ("thousands of parallel single-agent training envs; tens of interacting agents per scene today")
- Runway: "is this on our roadmap for v2?" — frame honestly as a research bet, not next-quarter work

**Time budget:** 12 minutes.
