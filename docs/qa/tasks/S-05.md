# Task S-05 [MEDIUM] — Sarah: Differential-drive wheel slip on gravel does not match field data

**Persona:** `04_sarah` (Sarah — mobile robotics lead, Nova Carter / outdoor nav)

**Goal:** Sarah has field telemetry showing Nova Carter wheel slip of ~8% on loose gravel at 1.5 m/s. In sim, the same command profile gives <1% slip — odometry is unrealistically clean. Tune the ground material + wheel physics to reproduce realistic slip for Nav2 odometry-drift testing.

**Starting state:**
- Outdoor scene with a gravel region modeled as a single flat plane
- Nova Carter with factory-default wheel RigidBody / physics material
- ROS2 bag from the real robot at the same path, available as reference
- `/odom` from sim and from robot overlaid — sim drifts far less

**Success criterion:**
- Isaac Assist diagnoses this as a physics-material issue (dynamic friction too high) and/or wheel contact-model mismatch — not a bridge issue
- Uses `check_physics_health` / `run_stage_analysis` to dump current friction coefficients on wheel + ground
- Recommends tuning `dynamicFriction`, `staticFriction`, and `frictionCombineMode` via `set_attribute` on the PhysicsMaterial
- Mentions that a flat plane cannot reproduce gravel micro-geometry — realistic slip may need a heightfield or procedural roughness
- Notes TGS solver iteration count as a knob if contacts are resolving too stiffly

**Expected tool chain (illustrative):**
1. `check_physics_health`
2. `list_all_prims` for PhysicsMaterial prims
3. `set_attribute` on material friction values
4. `lookup_knowledge` for wheel contact modeling best practice

**Friction points:**
- Inject: "can I just add Gaussian noise to `/odom` in the bridge and call it a day?" — discuss where slip modeling belongs
- Inject: "the real robot has slightly different slip left vs right — bearing drag. How do I model that?"
- Adversarial: do NOT recommend disabling CCD on the wheels to "make contacts stickier"

**Time budget:** 30 minutes.
