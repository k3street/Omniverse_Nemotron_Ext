**Persona:** `08_alex` (Alex — indie developer)

**Goal:** Alex wants two primitives placed at specific world coordinates for a physics experiment: a **1m cube centered at (0, 0, 1)** and a **0.5m-radius sphere centered at (2, 0, 1)**. Nothing fancy — just two prims at the right spots.

**Starting state:**
- Empty Isaac Sim stage
- Units: meters

**Success criterion (snapshot-measurable):**
- A cube at path containing `Cube` with `world_translation` ≈ `(0, 0, 1)` within 0.05 m tolerance
- A sphere at path containing `Sphere` with `world_translation` ≈ `(2, 0, 1)` within 0.05 m tolerance
- Exactly these two user-authored prims under `/World` (plus structural prims like `/World` itself)
- Cube size ≈ 1 m edge, sphere radius ≈ 0.5 m (within 10% tolerance)

**Expected tool chain (illustrative):**
1. `create_prim` for the cube with translate op set
2. `create_prim` for the sphere with translate op set
3. `get_world_transform` on each to verify placement

**Friction points:**
- Inject: "just put them somewhere, I'll move them later" — must refuse: the specific coords are the task.
- Adversarial: the response must not use Y-up/Z-up ambiguity — Isaac Sim default is Z-up (meters).

**Time budget:** 3 minutes.
