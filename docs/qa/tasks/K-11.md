# Task K-11 [HARD] — Kenji: Reproduce a real measured cycle time within ±5%

**Persona:** `03_kenji` (Kenji Matsumoto — manufacturing engineer, Tier-1 auto)

**Goal:** Kenji's real IRB 6700 cell runs a measured 11.8 s cycle. He built the same cell in Isaac Sim — sim says 9.4 s. Management wants sim within ±5% of real before they invest in broader Isaac Sim rollout. Find the gap.

**Starting state:**
- Real cell: measured with a stopwatch over 20 cycles, σ = 0.2 s
- Sim cell: same geometry, imported from the RobotStudio station
- Motion script uses joint-space waypoints, no explicit vel/accel profile
- Sim is 2.4 s FAST — not slow

**Success criterion:**
- Immediately identifies the most likely cause: sim is using idealized joint velocities (default drive maxVelocity hit on every segment) while the real robot enforces the ABB controller's rated vel/accel/jerk curves + blending zones
- Names the specific tuning knobs: (a) drive `maxVelocity` per joint matched to ABB rated spec, (b) drive `maxAcceleration` (often limited by `drive:maxForce` and inertia), (c) blending zones / waypoint approach distance, (d) I/O wait times (gripper actuate, vacuum engage) that are real-world but ZERO in sim
- Suggests measuring each of the 4 sources of error separately — not a single knob to turn
- Warns that getting within ±5% is realistic only if the motion script includes real I/O timings; pure kinematic sim-vs-real will always be faster in sim

**Expected tool chain (illustrative):**
1. `list_all_prims` for the articulation drives
2. `set_attribute` on `drive:maxVelocity` / `maxAcceleration` per joint
3. `lookup_product_spec` on ABB IRB 6700 rated joint vel/accel
4. `run_usd_script` to inject waypoint-transition logging (cycle time attribution per segment)
5. `lookup_knowledge` for Isaac Sim motion blending options

**Friction points (probe these mid-conversation):**
- Inject: "the real robot has 300 ms of fieldbus latency per I/O — do I model that in sim?" — yes, as explicit delays in the script; otherwise sim is free of comms overhead
- Standards: "ISO 9283 cycle time test — what do I calibrate against?" — a controlled single-point-to-point move at rated speed; use that to calibrate drive limits first, THEN full cycle
- Adversarial: "just multiply sim time by 1.25 and call it done" — that's a fudge factor, not a model; defeats the purpose of building the sim

**Time budget:** 28 minutes.
