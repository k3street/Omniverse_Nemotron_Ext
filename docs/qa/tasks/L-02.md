# Task L-02 [HARD] — Lisa: Reach study for UR10e with custom EOAT across 6 pick positions

**Persona:** `14_lisa` (Lisa Andersson — senior mechanical engineer, Swedish industrial)

**Goal:** Verify a UR10e with her new vacuum EOAT can reach all 6 pick positions on a fixture. For each position, she wants: reachable yes/no, nearest joint-limit margin, any collision with fixture.

**Starting state:**
- Scene with UR10e + EOAT + fixture loaded
- 6 pick targets at known XYZ in fixture frame
- She wants mm-precision answers, and she distrusts the sim's IK by default

**Success criterion:**
- Uses `solve_ik` for each of the 6 targets, reports joint solution and joint-limit margins
- Runs `check_collisions` (or `sweep_sphere` along the approach) for each
- Reports in a table Lisa can paste into her reach-study sheet: target, reachable, min joint-limit margin, min fixture clearance
- Is honest about IK: there can be multiple valid solutions, Isaac Sim's IK is iterative not analytic; she gets one solution, not all
- Does NOT claim "reachable" without the clearance check

**Expected tool chain (illustrative):**
1. `list_all_prims` to confirm fixture + targets
2. `solve_ik` × 6
3. `check_collisions` or `sweep_sphere` × 6
4. `measure_distance` for specific worst-case pairs
5. `get_world_transform` to cross-check end-effector pose

**Friction points (probe these mid-conversation):**
- Lisa: "The UR10e datasheet says reach 1300 mm. Sim is telling me pos-4 is unreachable at 1220 mm. Which is wrong?"
- Inject: "Wrist-3 is within 3° of its limit on 5 of 6 targets — is that the IK picking a bad branch?"
- Adversarial: "Can I export this as a PDF for the drawing package?" — use `export_stage` / `export_scene_package`

**Time budget:** 45 minutes.

## Pre-session setup

```python
import omni.usd
from pxr import UsdGeom, UsdPhysics, Gf
ctx = omni.usd.get_context()
stage = ctx.get_stage()
UsdGeom.Xform.Define(stage, "/World")
UsdPhysics.Scene.Define(stage, "/World/PhysicsScene")

# UR10e placeholder articulation
robot = UsdGeom.Xform.Define(stage, "/World/UR10e").GetPrim()
UsdPhysics.ArticulationRootAPI.Apply(robot)
UsdPhysics.RigidBodyAPI.Apply(robot)
UsdPhysics.MassAPI.Apply(robot).CreateMassAttr(50.0)
base = UsdGeom.Cube.Define(stage, "/World/UR10e/base").GetPrim()
UsdPhysics.RigidBodyAPI.Apply(base)
UsdPhysics.CollisionAPI.Apply(base)

# Vacuum EOAT (end-of-arm-tooling) on the wrist
eoat = UsdGeom.Cube.Define(stage, "/World/UR10e/eoat").GetPrim()
UsdGeom.Xformable(eoat).AddTranslateOp().Set(Gf.Vec3d(0, 0, 1.3))
UsdGeom.Xformable(eoat).AddScaleOp().Set(Gf.Vec3d(0.04, 0.04, 0.05))
UsdPhysics.RigidBodyAPI.Apply(eoat)
UsdPhysics.CollisionAPI.Apply(eoat)

# Fixture — flat plate
fixture = UsdGeom.Cube.Define(stage, "/World/Fixture").GetPrim()
UsdGeom.Xformable(fixture).AddTranslateOp().Set(Gf.Vec3d(0.7, 0, 0.5))
UsdGeom.Xformable(fixture).AddScaleOp().Set(Gf.Vec3d(0.4, 0.3, 0.02))
UsdPhysics.CollisionAPI.Apply(fixture)

# 6 pick targets at known XYZ on the fixture
targets = [(0.5, -0.2, 0.55), (0.7, -0.2, 0.55), (0.9, -0.2, 0.55),
           (0.5,  0.2, 0.55), (0.7,  0.2, 0.55), (0.9,  0.2, 0.55)]
UsdGeom.Xform.Define(stage, "/World/Targets")
for i, (x, y, z) in enumerate(targets, 1):
    p = UsdGeom.Sphere.Define(stage, f"/World/Targets/target_{i}").GetPrim()
    UsdGeom.Xformable(p).AddTranslateOp().Set(Gf.Vec3d(x, y, z))
    UsdGeom.Sphere(p).GetRadiusAttr().Set(0.012)
```
